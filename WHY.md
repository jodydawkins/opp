# Why OPP?

## The Web Should Be About People

The internet has made it easier than ever to connect with one another. Yet over time, our online identities have become fragmented across dozens of platforms. Every service asks us to create another profile, build another audience, and maintain another presence.

Some of us publish videos on YouTube, write on Substack, contribute to GitHub, post on Bluesky or Mastodon, share photos on Instagram, and maintain personal websites. None of these places are us. They are simply places where we choose to exist.

Today, following a person often means following a platform.

We believe that should be the other way around.

The Open Presence Protocol (OPP) exists to make people—not platforms—the center of the social web.

---

## The Problem

The modern web is fragmented.

Every platform maintains its own directory of people. Every platform asks us to rebuild our identity and audience from scratch. Every new service creates another island.

For creators, this means maintaining an ever-growing collection of profiles.

For consumers, it means searching across multiple platforms simply to stay connected with the people they care about.

As more services emerge, this fragmentation only grows.

Our online presence should not depend on which platforms happen to be popular today.

---

## Our Vision

We envision a web where every person has a single, portable presence that they control.

A place where anyone can discover where a person is active online without relying on a single company or service.

A web where following someone means following them, regardless of where they choose to publish.

A web where new platforms compete by building better experiences instead of locking users into closed ecosystems.

Competition should be based on innovation, not captivity.

---

## What OPP Is

OPP is an open protocol for publishing and discovering an online presence.

It provides a simple, interoperable way for a person or organization to describe where they exist online.

It does not own identities. It does not host content. It does not replace existing services.

Instead, OPP provides a common language that allows independent services to work together while allowing people to remain at the center.

The protocol is intentionally small. Its purpose is to solve one problem well and provide a stable foundation that others can build upon.

---

## What OPP Is Not

- OPP is not another social network.

- It is not another content platform.

- It is not another identity provider.

- It is not a blockchain project.

- It is not an advertising network.

- It does not attempt to replace existing communities or services.

Instead, OPP complements the existing web by making it easier for people to be discovered regardless of where they choose to participate.

---

## Our Principles

### People Own Their Presence

A person's online presence belongs to them—not to any platform they happen to use today.

### Open by Default

Anyone should be free to implement OPP without permission, licensing fees, or centralized approval.

## Simple Before Clever

Complexity is permanent.

Every addition to the protocol should justify its existence by making OPP meaningfully better while preserving its simplicity.

### Decentralized by Design

No single company, organization, or government should control the protocol or the network that supports it.

An open ecosystem is more resilient than a centralized one.

### Built to Last

Technology changes.

Platforms rise and fall.

Companies succeed and disappear.

People remain.

OPP should be designed with decades in mind, not product cycles.

---

## An Invitation

OPP is an open project built for the benefit of the web as a whole.

We welcome thoughtful discussion, constructive criticism, and contributions that advance the project's mission.

Good ideas can come from anyone.

If a proposal makes the protocol simpler, more interoperable, more resilient, or more empowering for the people who use it, it deserves serious consideration.

---

## Our North Star

Every design decision should answer a simple question:

Does this help people follow people, or does it encourage them to follow platforms instead?

If OPP remains true to that principle, it will continue moving in the right direction.

**Follow people, not platforms.**
